    Laura Caron and Erwin Tiongson,

    Some countries measure well-being, including inequalities in well-being, by consumption. But when people no longer travel to work or eat in restaurants because of COVID, the assumed relationship between spending and well-being – already loose, because of the poverty penalty – breaks down.

    Disability, employment and wages: evidence from Indonesia (2020)


    Laura Caron,

    International Journal of Manpower

    This paper quanitifies the labor market outcomes of people with disabilities (PwD) in Indonesia and compares them to people without disabilities. It distinguishes between medically-diagnosed disabilites and lived-experience disabilities to understand differences in access to diagnosis. This paper finds compelling evidence that, where a wage penalty exists, a substantial part is unexplained by observable characteristics.

    Migration and Mental Health in Mexico: Domestic Migrants, Return U.S. Migrants, and Non-Migrants (2020)


    Katharine Donato, Laura Caron, and Erin Hamilton,

    Frontiers in Psychiatry
